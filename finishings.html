Matte vs Glossy Paper: Which one is right for your project?

When it comes to printing, one of the most common questions is: Should I use matte or glossy paper? Each finish has its strengths, and the best choice depends on what you’re printing, how you want it to look, and how it’ll be used.

Glossy Paper

Glossy paper has a smooth, reflective coating that enhances color and contrast. It is ideal for images that need to pop.

  • Best for: Photos, Marketing flyers, Brochures with visuals, Product packaging
  • Pros: Richer color vibrancy, Sharper image detail, Professional finish
  • Cons: Prone to fingerprints and glare, Difficult to write on, Not ideal for text-heavy documents

Matte Paper

Matte paper has a non-reflective, flat finish that gives it a clean and refined look. It is easier to read and write on.

  • Best for: Resumes and cover letters, Art prints, Invitations, Text-heavy documents
  • Pros: No glare, Easier to handle and read, Smooth premium feel
  • Cons: Slightly muted colors, Less "wow" factor for image prints

Pro Tip: Think About Lighting

Glossy paper looks great in natural light, but can create glare under harsh indoor lighting. Matte paper remains consistent in any setting.
